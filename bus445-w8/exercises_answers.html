(2) Comparison of estimation and validation lifts

Answer: On the estimation data, the full-predictor neural network models (especially NN.FullHL4 and NN.FullHL6 with more hidden units) tend to show higher lift than MixedCCS2, because the additional predictor variables and the flexibility of the neural network allow it to capture more complex patterns in the training data. However, on the validation data, the performance gap between the neural networks and MixedCCS2 narrows considerably, and in some cases MixedCCS2 may perform comparably or even better. This suggests that the neural networks with more hidden units are overfitting — they are fitting noise in the estimation data that does not generalize to unseen data. The simpler logistic regression model (MixedCCS2) proves to be more robust and stable across samples. This tells us that simply adding more predictors and complexity does not necessarily improve predictive performance on new data.

(3) Suggested next steps

Answer: These results suggest several possible next steps:

  • Regularization tuning: Experiment with higher values of the decay parameter to increase regularization and reduce overfitting in the neural network models.
  • Variable selection: Rather than including all predictors, use the insights from the random forest variable importance plots or stepwise selection to identify a smaller, more informative subset of predictors that improves validation performance.
  • Ensemble approaches: Combine predictions from the logistic regression, tree, random forest, and neural network models to see if an ensemble yields better and more stable lift on the validation set.
  • Cross-validation: Use cross-validation instead of a single estimation/validation split to get more reliable estimates of model performance and reduce sensitivity to the particular split.
